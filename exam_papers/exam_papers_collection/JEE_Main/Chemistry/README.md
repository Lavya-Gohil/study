## üìã Exam Pattern

### Paper Structure
- **Total Questions**: 30 (Chemistry section)
- **Section A**: 20 questions (compulsory)
- **Section B**: 10 questions (attempt any 5)
- **Total Marks**: 100 (for Chemistry)
- **Marking**: +4 correct, -1 incorrect

## üìö Section-wise Topics & Weightage

### PHYSICAL CHEMISTRY (40-45% weightage)

**High Priority Topics:**
1. **Chemical Kinetics** (3-4 questions)
   - Rate laws, order of reaction
   - Integrated rate equations
   - Arrhenius equation
   - Half-life calculations

2. **Equilibrium** (3-4 questions)
   - Chemical equilibrium (Kc, Kp)
   - Ionic equilibrium
   - Buffer solutions
   - Solubility product

3. **Electrochemistry** (2-3 questions)
   - Nernst equation
   - Conductance
   - Electrolysis
   - Batteries and fuel cells

4. **Thermodynamics** (2-3 questions)
   - Laws of thermodynamics
   - Gibbs free energy
   - Entropy calculations
   - Hess's law

5. **Mole Concept** (1-2 questions)
   - Stoichiometry
   - Percentage composition
   - Concentration terms

6. **Atomic Structure** (2 questions)
   - Quantum numbers
   - Electronic configuration
   - Periodic properties

7. **Solutions** (2 questions)
   - Colligative properties
   - Raoult's law
   - Henry's law

8. **Solid State** (1-2 questions)
   - Crystal lattices
   - Unit cells
   - Packing efficiency

9. **Surface Chemistry** (1 question)
   - Adsorption
   - Colloids

### INORGANIC CHEMISTRY (30-35% weightage)

**High Priority Topics:**
1. **Coordination Compounds** (2-3 questions)
   - IUPAC nomenclature
   - VBT and CFT
   - Isomerism
   - Magnetic properties

2. **d & f Block Elements** (2-3 questions)
   - Properties of transition metals
   - Lanthanoids and actinoids
   - Compounds and their properties

3. **p-Block Elements** (3-4 questions)
   - Group 15: N, P compounds
   - Group 16: O, S compounds
   - Group 17: Halogens
   - Group 18: Noble gases

4. **Chemical Bonding** (2 questions)
   - Hybridization
   - VSEPR theory
   - Molecular orbital theory
   - Hydrogen bonding

5. **s-Block Elements** (1-2 questions)
   - Alkali metals
   - Alkaline earth metals
   - Properties and compounds

6. **Metallurgy** (1-2 questions)
   - Extraction of metals
   - Refining processes

7. **Qualitative Analysis** (1 question)
   - Salt analysis
   - Detection of ions

8. **Environmental Chemistry** (1 question)
   - Pollution
   - Green chemistry

### ORGANIC CHEMISTRY (25-30% weightage)

**High Priority Topics:**
1. **Reaction Mechanisms** (2-3 questions)
   - SN1, SN2, E1, E2
   - Electrophilic addition
   - Nucleophilic addition
   - Free radical reactions

2. **Organic Compounds** (4-5 questions)
   - Alcohols, phenols, ethers
   - Aldehydes and ketones
   - Carboxylic acids and derivatives
   - Amines

3. **Aromatic Chemistry** (2 questions)
   - Benzene reactions
   - Electrophilic aromatic substitution
   - Directing effects

4. **GOC (General Organic Chemistry)** (2-3 questions)
   - Resonance and hyperconjugation
   - Inductive and mesomeric effects
   - Carbocation stability
   - Acidity and basicity

5. **Hydrocarbons** (1-2 questions)
   - Alkanes, alkenes, alkynes
   - Petroleum refining

6. **Biomolecules** (1-2 questions)
   - Carbohydrates
   - Proteins
   - Vitamins and enzymes

7. **Polymers** (1 question)
   - Types of polymers
   - Polymerization reactions

8. **Haloalkanes & Haloarenes** (1-2 questions)
   - Preparation and reactions
   - Nucleophilic substitution

## üí° Preparation Strategy

### Physical Chemistry
- **Focus**: Numerical problem-solving
- Practice calculations daily
- Master all formulas and derivations
- Speed is crucial here

### Inorganic Chemistry
- **Focus**: Memory + Logic
- Make colorful charts for trends
- Practice IUPAC nomenclature
- Understand periodic trends well

### Organic Chemistry
- **Focus**: Reaction mechanisms
- Create reaction flowcharts
- Practice name reactions (Cannizzaro, Aldol, etc.)
- Understand, don't memorize mechanisms

## üí° Section-wise Strategy

### During Exam
1. **Start with Inorganic**: Mostly fact-based, quick to solve
2. **Then Organic**: Moderate difficulty, good scoring
3. **Physical Last**: Time-consuming calculations

### Time Management
- Inorganic: 15-20 minutes
- Organic: 20-25 minutes
- Physical: 25-30 minutes
- Review: 5-10 minutes

---

## üîç Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not reading NCERT thoroughly
2. Ignoring inorganic chemistry (easiest scoring section!)
3. Memorizing organic reactions without understanding
4. Not practicing physical chemistry numericals daily
5. Calculation errors in numerical problems
