## üìã Exam Pattern (Chemistry Section)

**Total Questions**: 50
- **Section A**: 35 questions (compulsory)
- **Section B**: 15 questions (attempt any 10)
- **Total to Attempt**: 45 questions
- **Total Marks**: 180 marks
- **Recommended Time**: 50-55 minutes

**Marking**: +4 correct, -1 incorrect

## üìö Chapter-wise Weightage

### PHYSICAL CHEMISTRY (23-27% = 12-14 questions)

**High Priority Topics:**

1. **Chemical Kinetics** (2-3 questions)
   - Rate law, order of reaction
   - Integrated rate equations
   - Arrhenius equation
   - Half-life problems

2. **Equilibrium** (2-3 questions)
   - Law of mass action
   - Kc, Kp relationships
   - Le Chatelier's principle
   - Ionic equilibrium, buffer solutions
   - Common ion effect
   - Solubility product

3. **Electrochemistry** (2 questions)
   - Nernst equation
   - Conductance
   - Kohlrausch's law
   - Electrolytic cells, batteries

4. **Solutions** (2 questions)
   - Colligative properties (very important!)
   - Raoult's law
   - Van't Hoff factor
   - Osmotic pressure

5. **Thermodynamics** (1-2 questions)
   - First and second laws
   - Gibbs free energy
   - Entropy
   - Hess's law

6. **Mole Concept** (1 question)
   - Stoichiometry
   - Limiting reagent
   - Percentage composition

7. **Atomic Structure** (1 question)
   - Quantum numbers
   - Electronic configuration
   - Periodic properties

8. **Solid State** (1 question)
   - Crystal systems
   - Unit cells
   - Packing efficiency

9. **Surface Chemistry** (1 question)
   - Adsorption
   - Catalysis

### INORGANIC CHEMISTRY (30-35% = 15-18 questions)

**Highest Weightage Section in Chemistry!**

1. **Coordination Compounds** (2-3 questions)
   - IUPAC nomenclature (very important!)
   - Isomerism (geometrical, optical)
   - VBT, CFT basics
   - Magnetic properties

2. **d & f Block Elements** (2-3 questions)
   - Properties of transition metals
   - Oxidation states
   - Colored compounds
   - Lanthanoids, actinoids

3. **p-Block Elements** (4-5 questions) - **HIGHEST WEIGHTAGE**
   - Group 15 elements (N, P)
   - Group 16 elements (O, S)
   - Group 17 elements (Halogens)
   - Group 18 elements (Noble gases)
   - Compounds and their properties
   - Oxoacids

4. **Chemical Bonding** (2 questions)
   - Hybridization
   - VSEPR theory
   - Molecular orbital theory
   - Hydrogen bonding

5. **s-Block Elements** (2 questions)
   - Alkali metals
   - Alkaline earth metals
   - Compounds (hydroxides, carbonates, etc.)

6. **Qualitative Analysis** (1-2 questions)
   - Salt analysis
   - Identification of cations/anions

7. **Metallurgy** (1 question)
   - Extraction processes
   - Refining

8. **Hydrogen** (1 question)
   - Properties, isotopes
   - Hydrogen compounds

9. **Environmental Chemistry** (1 question)
   - Air/water pollution
   - Green chemistry

### ORGANIC CHEMISTRY (40-45% = 20-23 questions)

**Second Highest Weightage!**

1. **General Organic Chemistry (GOC)** (2-3 questions)
   - Inductive effect, resonance
   - Hyperconjugation
   - Electrophiles, nucleophiles
   - Carbocation stability
   - Acidity and basicity

2. **Reaction Mechanisms** (3-4 questions)
   - SN1, SN2 reactions
   - E1, E2 eliminations
   - Addition reactions
   - Substitution reactions

3. **Organic Compounds** (8-10 questions) - **CORE SCORING AREA**
   - **Alcohols, Phenols, Ethers** (2 questions)
     - Preparation methods
     - Physical/chemical properties
     - Reactions
   
   - **Aldehydes & Ketones** (2-3 questions)
     - Preparation
     - Nucleophilic addition
     - Reactions with NH2 derivatives
   
   - **Carboxylic Acids & Derivatives** (2 questions)
     - Preparation methods
     - Acidic nature
     - Reactions
   
   - **Amines** (1-2 questions)
     - Preparation
     - Basicity
     - Diazonium salts

4. **Aromatic Chemistry** (2-3 questions)
   - Benzene structure
   - Electrophilic aromatic substitution
   - Directing effects (o/p and meta)
   - Friedel-Crafts reactions

5. **Hydrocarbons** (1-2 questions)
   - Alkanes, alkenes, alkynes
   - Preparation and properties
   - Reactions

6. **Haloalkanes & Haloarenes** (2 questions)
   - Preparation methods
   - Reactions
   - Nucleophilic substitution

7. **Biomolecules** (2-3 questions)
   - Carbohydrates (monosaccharides, polysaccharides)
   - Proteins and amino acids
   - Enzymes, vitamins
   - Nucleic acids (DNA, RNA)

8. **Polymers** (1-2 questions)
   - Classification
   - Addition and condensation polymers
   - Important polymers

9. **Chemistry in Everyday Life** (1-2 questions)
   - Drugs and medicines
   - Cleansing agents
   - Food chemistry

## üí° NEET-Specific Preparation Strategy

### The NCERT Advantage
**70-75% questions are directly from NCERT!**
- Even more than Physics
- Read NCERT 3-4 times
- Don't skip any line
- Focus on tables, reaction schemes
- Named reactions are important

### Section-wise Focus

**Physical Chemistry**
- Formula-based, high calculation
- Practice numerical daily (20 problems)
- Make formula sheets
- Focus on mole concept basics

**Inorganic Chemistry**
- Memory-intensive but easy scoring
- Make colorful charts
- Create mnemonics for trends
- Focus on exceptions
- IUPAC nomenclature is crucial

**Organic Chemistry**
- Most scoring if prepared well
- Create reaction flowcharts
- Practice name reactions
- Learn mechanisms, don't memorize
- Functional group priority order

## üí° Section B Strategy (Chemistry)

### Choosing 10 from 15 Questions

**Approach**:
1. Scan all 15 questions quickly (2 minutes)
2. Categorize: Easy, Medium, Hard
3. Mark 10 easiest questions
4. Attempt only those 10
5. Don't attempt 11th or more (waste of time, no benefit)

**Common Topics in Section B**:
- Usually from less important chapters
- Often numerical problems
- Sometimes tricky NCERT lines

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not Reading NCERT Properly**
   - Biggest mistake in NEET Chemistry
   - Read every single line, table, diagram

2. **Ignoring Inorganic Chemistry**
   - Easiest scoring section (35% marks!)
   - Pure memory, no calculations
   - Just revise regularly

3. **Over-complicating Organic**
   - NEET asks basic reactions
   - Focus on NCERT reactions first
   - Don't dive into advanced mechanisms

4. **Not Practicing Numericals**
   - Physical chemistry has many numericals
   - Practice daily (even 10-15 problems)

5. **Poor IUPAC Nomenclature**
   - 2-3 questions guaranteed
   - Easy scoring if you know rules
   - Practice daily (5 compounds)

6. **Not Making Flowcharts**
   - Organic reactions need flowcharts
   - Helps in quick revision
   - Reduces confusion

## üéØ During NEET Exam (Chemistry)

### Time Management (55 minutes for Chemistry)

**0-3 min**: Scan all 50 questions
**3-30 min**: Solve Section A easy + medium (25-30 questions)
**30-40 min**: Solve Section A difficult (5-10 questions)
**40-52 min**: Section B (choose 10, solve them)
**52-55 min**: Review marked questions

### Attempt Order Recommendation

1. **Start with Inorganic** (10-12 min)
   - Fastest to solve
   - Confidence booster
   - Pure fact recall

2. **Then Organic** (20-25 min)
   - Good scoring potential
   - Moderate time per question
   - Usually straightforward

3. **Finally Physical** (15-18 min)
   - Time-consuming
   - Calculation-heavy
   - Tricky options
