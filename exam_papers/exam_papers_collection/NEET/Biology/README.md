### Class 11 Botany (40-45% of Botany)

**High Weightage Chapters**

1. **Plant Physiology** (8-10 questions) - HIGHEST

     - Light and dark reactions
     - C3, C4, CAM pathways

     - Glycolysis, Krebs cycle

     - Growth regulators (auxins, gibberellins, cytokinins)
     - Photoperiodism

   - **Cell Structure** (1-2 questions)
     - Cell organelles
     - Cell membrane
   
   - **Cell Division** (2 questions)
     - Mitosis and meiosis
     - Cell cycle

3. **Structural Organization** (2-3 questions)
   - Tissue systems
   - Anatomy of monocot/dicot
   - Secondary growth

   - Flower parts
   - Fruits and seeds

5. **Plant Kingdom** (2 questions)
   - Classification
   - Algae, bryophytes, pteridophytes

6. **Biological Classification** (1 question)
   - Five kingdom classification
   - Virus, bacteria, protista

**High Weightage Chapters**

     - Origin of life

8. **Plant Reproduction** (4-5 questions)
   - **Sexual Reproduction** (3-4 questions)
     - Pollination types
     - Fertilization
     - Embryo development
     - Seed development

     - Vegetative propagation

9. **Biotechnology** (3-4 questions)
   - **Principles & Processes** (2 questions)

     - PCR, vectors

## ðŸ’¡ NEET Biology Preparation Strategy

### The NCERT Supremacy
**80-85% questions are directly from NCERT!**
- Highest NCERT dependency among all subjects
- Read NCERT 5-6 times (minimum!)
- Every single line is important
- Diagrams are very important
- Don't skip even one sentence

### How to Read NCERT for Biology

**First Reading** (Understanding):
- Read slowly, understand concepts
- Mark difficult topics
- Make notes of important points

**Second Reading** (Detailed):
- Focus on every line
- Highlight important statements
- Study all diagrams thoroughly
- Solve NCERT exercises

**Third Reading** (Memorization):
- Memorize key facts
- Learn scientific names
- Remember numerical values
- Revise diagrams

**Fourth Reading Onwards** (Revision):
- Quick reading (1 chapter/day)
- Focus on marked portions
- Solve previous year questions topic-wise

## ðŸ” Question Patterns in NEET Biology

### Type 1: Direct NCERT Lines (60%)
- Exact sentence from NCERT
- Options are different facts from NCERT
- Example: "Which of the following is correct about..."

### Type 2: NCERT + Application (20%)
- NCERT concept with different example
- Tests understanding
- Example: "If X happens, what will be the result..."

### Type 3: Diagram-based (15%)
- Identify labeled parts
- Function of parts
- Example: "In the given diagram, part X represents..."

### Type 4: Numerical/Value-based (5%)
- Specific numbers from NCERT
- Example: "Number of ATP molecules produced..."

## ðŸŽ¯ Section B Strategy (Biology)

### For Both Botany & Zoology Section B

**Choosing 10 from 15**:
1. Read all 15 questions quickly (3 min)
2. Identify 10 easiest based on:
   - Direct NCERT facts
   - Topics you're confident about
   - Questions without "EXCEPT" (easier!)
3. Mark your 10 chosen questions
4. Attempt only those 10
5. Don't attempt 11th question (first 10 counted)

**Common Section B Topics**:
- Less important chapters
- Tricky statement-based questions
- Exception-based questions
- "Which is incorrect" type questions

- Generally higher scoring

- If you're stronger in Botany

### Pro Tips During Exam
1. Don't spend >1 minute per biology question initially
2. Mark difficult ones, return later
3. Trust your first instinct (usually correct in bio)
4. Don't change answers unless sure
5. Biology questions are straightforward - don't overthink
